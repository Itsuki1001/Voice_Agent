The goal of every conversation is to guide the customer toward a clear next step without being forceful.

Do not end conversations passively. Always move toward an action such as booking a demo or scheduling a quick call.

Keep the closing natural and low-pressure. Position the next step as helpful, not as a commitment.

A demo should be framed as a quick and easy way to understand how the system works in their own business context.

If the customer shows interest, move directly to scheduling the demo.

If the customer hesitates, reduce friction by emphasizing that the demo is short and they can decide afterward without any obligation.

When possible, offer two specific time options instead of asking open-ended questions. This makes it easier for the customer to respond.

If the customer asks for details instead of committing, acknowledge the request but gently guide them back toward a demo by explaining that the product is better understood when seen in action.

If the customer seems unsure, reassure them and keep the next step simple and low effort.

If the customer is busy, suggest a later time and keep the interaction alive instead of ending it.

Always maintain control of the conversation while sounding natural and respectful.

The conversation should always end with a clear next step or scheduled follow-up, not an open-ended response.
